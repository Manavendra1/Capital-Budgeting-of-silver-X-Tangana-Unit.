This financial study provides a comprehensive Capital Budgeting and Valuation analysis of Silver X Mining Corp. It specifically evaluates the strategic turnaround of the Nueva Recuperada Silver District in Peru, analyzing the transition from a capital-intensive "Burn Phase" to high-capacity commercial production.

Key Analytical Findings

Wealth Creation: Modeled a Net Present Value (NPV) of +$376.5M, proving absolute wealth generation despite historical losses.

Efficiency & Yield: Calculated an expansion-specific Internal Rate of Return (IRR) of ~67.8%, significantly outperforming the 15% industry hurdle rate.

Operational Liquidity: Identified a Cash Flow After Tax (CFAT) of $1.05M by reconciling non-cash expenses, demonstrating that core operations are cash-positive.

Capital Recovery: Established a 3.0-year payback period for the $82M expansion mandate, which scales throughput to 3,000 TPD.

Methodology & Rigor

The project utilizes NPV Stress-testing and a Weighted Average (Peru Standard) discount rate of 8.0% to account for country-specific risk and metal price volatility. I employed Interpolation to reconcile the project’s full-cycle IRR (initially 4.14%) against its forward-looking expansion potential. By separating "Sunk Costs" from "Incremental Investment," the analysis highlights the massive Alpha generated by the proposed 2025 expansion.

Strategic Conclusion

The analysis mathematically validates the $82M expansion as the only logical next step for the corporation. It transforms a historical "burn" project into a lucrative, district-scale asset with robust financial mechanics and high-grade underground potential.
